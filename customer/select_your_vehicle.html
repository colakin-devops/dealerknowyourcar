Gross Vehicle Mass (GVM) refers to the total maximum weight of a vehicle when fully loaded, as specified by the vehicle manufacturer. This includes the vehicle's own weight, passengers, cargo, and any additional accessories or equipment.

Gross Combined Mass (GCM) refers to the maximum allowable weight of a combination of vehicles, typically a towing vehicle (such as a truck or SUV) and its trailer, when fully loaded, as specified by the vehicle manufacturer.

Payload refers to the maximum weight a vehicle can carry, as specified by the vehicle manufacturer. It includes passengers and cargo, denoting the weight capacity available for additional items beyond the vehicle's own weight, such as luggage, equipment, or other goods.

Tow Capacity Braked refers to the maximum weight a vehicle can tow when the trailer being towed is equipped with its own braking system, as specified by the vehicle manufacturer. This capacity indicates the allowable tow weight for trailers that have brakes independent of the towing vehicle's braking system.

Towball download, also known as tongue weight or hitch weight, refers to the downward force exerted on the tow vehicle's towball or hitch by the tongue of a trailer or caravan when connected for towing, as specified by the vehicle manufacturer.

Kerb weight, also known as curb weight, refers to the total weight of a vehicle when it is unoccupied and ready for use, as specified by the vehicle manufacturer. This includes all fluids, fuel, and standard equipment, but excludes any passengers or cargo.
